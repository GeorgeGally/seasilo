Sea Silos

By George Gally - @radarboy3000
The earth's histroy has always been intrinsicly linked to the sea. Ships meant military and economic superiority. They meant migration, colonisation, and they brought explorers, exploiters and traders, and made the world a much smaller place.
And now the sea now brings new changes. A simple 20ft metal box. The Twenty-Foot Equivalent Unit (TEU) shipping container. The single greatest driver of globalisation and everything it brings with it, the goodies and the badies.
95% of everything we own and use, at some point, travels to us, on our seas. An industry and network of ocean routes we know very little about. This is the shipping industry, with the sea as their highway, sailing us into a beautiful and scary technolgical and industrial hyper-revolution. Now sit back, and contemplate the enormity of it all...
"These ships and boxes belong to a business that feeds, clothes, warms, and supplies us. They have fueled if not created globalization. They are the reason behind your cheap T-shirt and reasonably priced television. But who looks behind a television now and sees the ship that brought it? Who cares about the men who steered your breakfast cereal through winter storms? How ironic that the more ships have grown in size and consequence, the less space they take up in our imagination."
Rose George
